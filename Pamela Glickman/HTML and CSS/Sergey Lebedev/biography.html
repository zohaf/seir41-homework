Sergey Lebedev: Biography

Sergey Alexeyevich Lebedev (not to be confused with Sergey Nikolayevich Lebedev, the Russian politician or Sergey Vasilievich Lebedev, the Soviet chemist, who invented the first mass-produced type of synthetic rubber) was the father of the Soviet computer industry.

Early Life

He was born November 2nd 1902 in the city of Nizhny Novgorod, located in Central Russia, near the intersection of the Oka and Volga rivers. He graduated from the Moscow Highest Technical School in 1928 and received a Doctor of Sciences (Russia's version of a PhD) in 1939.

Career

During World War II, he developed an analog computer system that could solve differential equations, helping to automatic the Soviet military. After the war, he learned that Western scientists were developing electronic computers in secret and decided to build his own.

Sergey went on to develop a series of increasingly powerful computers, which you can read about here. From 1953 onwards he was the director of the Soviet Academy of Sciences, which is now named the Lebedev Institute of Precision Mechanics and Computer Engineering (IPMCE).

Legacy

He died in Moscow on July 3rd 1974. In 1996, he was posthumously given a Computer Pioneer Award from the IEEE Computer Society.
